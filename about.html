In the real world

I'm professional web developer. I write code for free snacks, coffee, the occassional pizza and the least amount of money possible. I guess it could be worse...

Seriously though, it's great. I've done some interesting and rewarding work. Much of the code I blog about here, originated from larger projects at my day job.

I've been fortunate to work with a creative and talented team of developers and web deisgners. Any project, no matter seemingly mundane, can be elevated

I started late in life, after decades working in an entirely different industry.

AKA Scrawlon

What is a Scrawlon? Is it a slang? Does it mean something rude???? No. No. No. It was originally the name of long defunct web application I created -- kind of a cross between Twitter and Evernote, if you can imagine. Anyway, I always liked the sound of it. It's short and memorable, like a fictional computer hacker (Neo, Count Zero, Mr. Robot). Scrawlon has been my Internet moniker for many years now.
