A brief bit about me: I studied journalism at university. I work in the engineering dept. at a network television station.

I've always wanted to be a programmer. Ever since we were allowed to tinker with BASIC in junior high computer class. Unfortunately, at that time in history computer programming was only offered to the most mathematically-inclined whiz-kids. I and most others were barred from the computer lab soon after. It wasn't for the rest of us.

I believed that until the recent appearance of free online education sites like:

  • MIT Open Courseware
  • Coursera
  • edX
  • P2PU

Thankfully, I and probably millions of others have found and explored an interest in programming this way. It's an awesome time to be a learner, but before these self-contained, auto-graded, video-lectured virtual class rooms sprouted out of the modern internet, there were individuals writing about their trials and triumphs and sharing useful tutorials - bloggers. I can't count the number of times I've been rescued from frustration by the writings of some unknown programmer found on 'the Google'. I can only hope that something I write here will have the same impact for anyone.
